Margaret Ekpo at her prime

Life of Margaret Ekpo

Margaret Ekpo was born on 27 June, 1914 in Creek Town, Calabar in Cross River state into the family of Inyang Eyo Aniemewue from the Royal Stock of King Eyo Honesty II and Okoroafor Obiasulor native of Agulu-Uzo-Igbo near Awka in Anambra State. She reached standard six of the school-leaving certificate in 1934 but she could not further her education to secondary school level because she lost her husband in the same year. Her goal to further education in teacher's training was also put on hold. She then started working as a pupil teacher in elementery schools. In 1946, she had the oppourtunity to study abroad at what is now Dublin Ireland. She earned a diploma in Domestic Science and on her return to Nigeria she established a Domestic Science and Sewing Institute in Aba. She was a Nigerian women's right activist who became the first female politician in the country's first republic. She played major roles as a grassroot and nationalist politician in the Eastern Nigerian City of Aba.

Achievements of Margaret Ekpo

  • In 1950s, she joined Fumilayo Ransome Kuti to protest killings at an Enugu coal mine, the victims were leaders protesting colonial practises at the mine.
  • In 1953, Ekpo was nominated by the NCNC to the regional House of Chiefs.
  • In 1954, she established the Aba Township Women's Association.
  • In 1961, she won a seat at the Eastern Regional House of Assembly, a position that allowed her to fight for economic and political issues affecting women.
  • She founded NCNC women's wing

Death of Margaret Ekpo

Margaret Ekpo died on Thursday, September 21, 2006, at the University of Calabar Teaching Hospital, Calabar, Cross River State. She was 92 years.
